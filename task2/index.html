Tribute to R. Praggnanandhaa

Rameshbabu Praggnanandhaa is an Indian chess prodigy. He is one of the youngest grandmasters in the history of chess, achieving the title at the age of 12 years, 10 months, and 13 days.

About Praggnanandhaa

Born on August 10, 2005, in Chennai, Tamil Nadu, Praggnanandhaa and his sister Vaishali are the first brother-sister duo to both earn the GM title. They are also the first brother-sister duo to qualify for the Candidates.

Personal Life

Praggnanandhaa was born in Chennai, Tamil Nadu. His father, Rameshbabu, works as a branch manager at TNSC Bank, and his mother, Nagalakshmi, is a homemaker. His elder sister, R Vaishali, is also a chess grandmaster.

Career

2013–2017

Praggnanandhaa won the World Youth Chess Championship Under-8 title in 2013, earning him the title of FIDE Master. He won the under-10 title in 2015. In 2016, he became the youngest international master in history at the age of 10 years, 10 months, and 19 days. He achieved his first grandmaster norm at the World Junior Chess Championship in November 2017, finishing fourth with 8 points.

2018

Praggnanandhaa gained his second GM norm at the Heraklion Fischer Memorial GM norm tournament in Greece on 17 April 2018. On 23 June 2018, he achieved his third and final norm at the Gredine Open in Urtijëi, Italy, becoming the then second-youngest person ever to achieve the rank of grandmaster. In 2018, he was invited to the Magistral de León Masters in Spain for a four-game rapid match against Wesley So.

2019

In July 2019, Praggnanandhaa won the Xtracon Chess Open in Denmark, scoring 8½/10 points. On 12 October 2019, he won the World Youth Championships in the Under-18 section with a score of 9/11. In December 2019, he became the second-youngest person to achieve a rating of 2600 at the age of 14 years, 3 months and 24 days.

2021

In April 2021, Praggnanandhaa won the Polgar Challenge, the first leg of the Julius Baer Challengers Chess Tour. This win helped him qualify for the Meltwater Champions Chess Tour. He entered the Chess World Cup 2021 and advanced to round 4, where he was eliminated by Maxime Vachier-Lagrave.

2022

Praggnanandhaa played in the Masters section of the Tata Steel Chess Tournament 2022, finishing in 12th place. He was part of the India-2 team in the 44th Chess Olympiad, which won the bronze medal. He also defeated World Champion Magnus Carlsen in the Airthings Masters rapid tournament and the Chessable Masters online rapid chess tournament.

2023

In January 2023, Praggnanandhaa played in the Tata Steel Chess Masters 2023 and ended the tournament in 9th place. In the Chess World Cup 2023, he became the world's youngest player to reach the Chess World Cup final, securing second place and qualifying for the 2024 Candidates Tournament.

2024

Praggnanandhaa placed 5th in the 2024 Candidates Tournament and defeated Magnus Carlsen for the first time in a classical 'over the board' match in the Norway Chess 2024 tournament.

Achievements

  • Youngest International Master in 2016
  • Grandmaster at the age of 12 years, 10 months, and 13 days
  • Winner of multiple international chess tournaments
  • Second-youngest person to achieve a rating of 2600
  • First brother-sister duo with sister Vaishali to both earn the GM title
  • Youngest player to reach the Chess World Cup final
  • Multiple wins against World Champion Magnus Carlsen
